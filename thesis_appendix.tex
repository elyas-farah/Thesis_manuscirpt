\chapter{Sensitivity of the Gas Power Spectrum to Parameter Variations}%
\label{sec:app_sensitivity}
%==============================================================================


I present the sensitivity to the four cosmological parameters: the baryon density $\Omega_\mathrm{b}$, the cold dark matter density $\Omega_\mathrm{c}$, the amplitude of matter fluctuations $\sigma_8$, and the reduced Hubble constant $h$, followed by the baryonic feedback parameters.

\section*{Cosmological Parameters}
%------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    \centering
    \begin{minipage}[t]{0.48\textwidth}
        \centering
        \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figs/p_Omega_b.png}
        \caption{Sensitivity of the gas power spectrum to the baryon density parameter $\Omega_\mathrm{b}$. Higher baryon fractions increase the overall gas content, boosting power on all scales.}

    \begin{minipage}[t]{0.48\textwidth}

        \label{fig:p_Omega_c}
    \end{minipage}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[htbp]
    \centering
    \begin{minipage}[t]{0.48\textwidth}
        \centering
        \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figs/p_sigma8.png}
        \caption{Sensitivity of the gas power spectrum to $\sigma_8$, the RMS amplitude of linear matter fluctuations on $8\,h^{-1}\,\mathrm{Mpc}$ scales. Larger $\sigma_8$ enhances structure formation and increases power at small scales.}
